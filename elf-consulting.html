e.l.f. Cosmetics: What Needs to Change

Team Project (5 members)

Developed a consulting-style recommendation for e.l.f. Cosmetics, arguing that sustainability should become a stronger source of differentiation in a highly competitive and price-sensitive beauty market.

FrameworksIndustry analysis, VRIN, activity system
ProblemDifferentiate in a saturated market
RecommendationSustainability-led repositioning

Overview

This project analyzed how e.l.f. could strengthen long-term brand differentiation while maintaining its mass-market accessibility. The argument centered on sustainability as a strategic lever that could be operationalized across sourcing, innovation, marketing, and customer engagement.

What I Did

  • Analyzed the competitive structure of the mass-market cosmetics industry
  • Evaluated e.l.f.'s activity system and strategic fit
  • Used VRIN logic to assess sustainability as a defendable resource
  • Developed a repositioning recommendation focused on affordable sustainable beauty
  • Outlined risks involving margin pressure and supply-chain complexity
